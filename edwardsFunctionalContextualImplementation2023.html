Transdisciplinary paper arguing that evolution does not select brains towards veridicality, but rather towards evolutionary fitness. The abstract claims to tie it in with predictive processing ideas.

Questions before I read:

  • is truth fit?

  • how are you characterising fitness? in what environments?

  • do you narrow your idea of fitness enough to be computationally useful? (I'd be surprised. this is too broad a paper.)

  • Related concepts for which I'd like to clarify the relationship further:

    • predictive processing

    • satisficing

    • adversarial neural networks

    • coevolution

    • exaptation (in particular, because of the way the introduction discusses the tapetum lucidum; and also because I think exaptation is cool and useful)
